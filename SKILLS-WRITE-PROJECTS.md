# Skills: Write Portfolio Project Case Studies

> **Purpose:** This document is a prompt/skills file for Claude, GPT, or any AI agent to write project case studies for Tien Duong's creative portfolio. It encodes voice, structure, frameworks, and anti-patterns — all derived from research into how top creative portfolios actually write.

## 1. WHO IS TIEN DUONG — THE VOICE

### Identity

- Currently Account Executive at Red Agency JSC
- Past: Creative Executive at VuVer.vn, founded Pixself (D2C keychain brand)

### Voice Characteristics
| Trait | Do This | Never Do This |
|-------|---------|---------------|

| **Conversational** | Short sentences. Questions. Em dashes. | Academic prose or marketing jargon |

### Tone Calibration
```
Corporate ←——————————————————→ Casual
              ↑ HERE (approx 65% casual)
```

Tien's voice sits between a smart friend explaining their work over coffee and a professional case study. It's **never stiff**, but it's **never sloppy**. Think: the smart kid who explains complex strategy in simple words because they actually understand it.

### Language Patterns Tien Uses

## 2. THE FRAMEWORK — NOT STAR, BUT "HUMAN JOURNEY"

### Why Not Pure STAR?

STAR (Situation → Task → Action → Result) works for interviews but reads **robotic** in creative portfolios. The top creative portfolios (Kien Nguyen, Ho Gia Hoang, VCU Brandcenter graduates) use a **narrative arc** instead.

### Research Summary: Portfolio Writing Frameworks

| Framework | Origin | Structure | Best For |
|-----------|--------|-----------|----------|
| **STAR** | Behavioral interviews | Situation → Task → Action → Result | Job interviews, quick answers |
| **CAR/CARR** | Career coaching | Challenge → Action → Result → Reflection | Quick case studies with learning emphasis |
| **PAR/PARI** | Senior portfolios | Problem → Action → Result → Impact | ROI-focused, senior roles |
| **Design Journey** | UX community | Hook → Conflict → Process → Resolution | UX/product design portfolios |
| **Ad Case Study** | Agency world | Problem → Insight → Idea → Execution → Results | Creative advertising portfolios |
| **3-Act Narrative** | Storytelling | Context/Tension → Creative Conflict → Resolution | Human-first, leadership portfolios |

### The Framework Tien's Portfolio Actually Uses: "Human Journey"

Based on analysis of all 6 existing projects in `projects.json`, Tien's case studies follow a hybrid structure that combines the **advertising case study** (insight-driven) with **3-Act Narrative** (human-first) and adds a **reflection layer** from CAR:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  1. THE TAGLINE (question format)                   │
│     → Emotional hook, not a project title            │
│                                                     │
│  2. THE CHALLENGE (the real problem)                │
│     → Reframe the client brief into human terms      │
│                                                     │
│  3. THE INSIGHT (the "aha")                         │
│     → One sentence. The cultural/emotional truth.    │
│                                                     │
│  4. THE APPROACH (the strategic shift)              │
│     → What we decided to do differently, and why     │
│                                                     │
│  5. THE EXECUTION (what we actually did)            │
│     → 3-5 bullets. Specific, verb-led actions.       │
│                                                     │
│  6. THE RESULTS (honest outcomes)                   │
│     → Mix of metrics + qualitative learning          │
│     → Always end with "What I/we learned" row        │
│                                                     │
│  7. THE STORY (optional deep-dive blocks)           │
│     → Headings, body text, images, quotes            │
│     → Only for featured/complex projects             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Why This Framework Works for Tien

| Aspect | Reason |
|--------|--------|
| Leads with emotion (tagline) | Hooks the reader before they see any details |
| Reframes the brief (challenge) | Proves strategic thinking, not just task completion |
| Has an insight layer | Separates Tien from portfolios that are just "here's what I did" |
| Ends with reflection (results) | Shows maturity — rare for early-career portfolios |
| Optional story blocks | Lets featured projects go deep without forcing every project into the same length |

### Comparison: Tien's Human Journey vs. Other Frameworks

| Framework | Tien Uses? | Notes |
|-----------|------------|-------|
| **STAR** | ❌ Too rigid | Reads like a form, not a story |
| **CAR** | ⚠️ Partially | Good but missing the insight layer |
| **PAR** | ⚠️ Partially | Good but too metrics-focused for early career |
| **Design Journey** | ✅ Closest | Tien's version is more emotion-led |
| **Ad Case Study** | ✅ Core model | This is the base, with personal reflection layered on |
| **Human Journey** | ✅ **Primary** | Hybrid of ad case study + personal reflection |

## 3. FIELD-BY-FIELD WRITING GUIDE

### 3.1 `tagline` — The Emotional Hook

**Format:** A question or provocative statement that frames the human tension, not the deliverable.

**Rules:**
- Always a question or a "what happens when…" statement
- Never mention the brand first
- Focus on the *human feeling*, not the business KPI
- Under 15 words

**Examples from existing projects:**
| ✅ Good (Tien's actual taglines) | ❌ Bad (what to avoid) |
|---|---|
| "Why do people carry tiny versions of their pets on their bags?" | "Custom pixel-art keychain brand launch" |
| "The quiet panic of finding a gift that says 'I actually know you'" | "Valentine's Day e-commerce campaign" |
| "What happens when young people feel disconnected from their own heritage?" | "Cultural heritage marketing strategy" |
| "The anxiety of being a first-time parent buying things you don't understand" | "Gen Z mom targeting strategy for sports brand" |
| "How do you make 'sustainable banking' mean something to a normal person?" | "BIDV ESG communication strategy" |
| "Learning by watching — my first professional production set" | "Production assistant role at AEON" |

**Template:**
```
[Emotional state / question] + [Relatable human situation]
```

---

### 3.2 `challenge` — Reframing the Brief

**Format:** 2-4 sentences. Start with the client's stated problem, then pivot to the *real* human problem underneath.

**Structure pattern Tien uses:**
```
[Client wanted X]. But when we [dug deeper / talked to people / started working],
we realized [the real problem was Y]. [Restate in human/emotional terms].
```

**Key Rules:**
- First sentence = the business/client framing
- Second sentence = "But…" pivot to the human truth
- Use conversational connectors: "But here's the thing…", "The problem:", "When we started talking to…"
- Include a cultural or local detail if relevant
- **Never use marketing jargon** (no "target segments", "brand positioning", "market penetration")

**Word count:** 40–80 words

**Example (Kingsport):**
> "Kingsport sells sports equipment for kids. They wanted to reach 'Gen Z moms.' But when we started talking to these women, we realized the brief missed the point. These aren't just 'consumers' — they're first-time parents terrified of making the wrong choice, drowning in conflicting advice, desperate to be seen as good mothers."

---

### 3.3 `insight` — The Cultural/Emotional Truth

**Format:** 1-2 sentences. The single "aha" moment that bridges the problem to the solution.

**Rules:**
- This is the **most important sentence** in the entire case study
- Must feel like a *discovery*, not an *assertion*
- Should make the reader pause and think "oh, that's true"
- Written as a universal human truth, not a data point
- **No citations needed** — this is strategic intuition, not academic writing

**Pattern:**
```
[Audience] doesn't want [what you'd assume]. They want [deeper emotional need].
```

**Examples from existing projects:**
| Project | Insight |
|---------|---------|
| Pixself | "In a world where everything is digital, people crave physical objects that anchor their identity." |
| Kingsport | "Gen Z moms don't trust brands. They've grown up seeing through marketing. But they desperately trust *each other*." |
| BIDV | "People want to do good. But they're exhausted by abstract promises. They need to see the thread between 'I opened this account' and 'this village got clean water.'" |
| Chuon Ngo | "Gen Z doesn't want to be lectured about tradition. But they desperately want to belong to something meaningful." |
| VuVer | "They're afraid of getting it wrong. Of giving a gift that says 'I don't really know you.'" |

**Word count:** 15–40 words

---

### 3.4 `approach` — The Strategic Shift

**Format:** 2-3 sentences. What you decided to do differently — and *why*.

**Rules:**
- Frame as a deliberate *shift* from the expected approach
- Use "We stopped…and started…" or "Instead of…we…" structure
- Show strategic thinking without being pretentious
- Keep it actionable, not philosophical

**Pattern:**
```
We stopped [conventional approach] and started [Tien's approach].
[One sentence explaining why this was the right move].
```

**Example (Chuon Ngo):**
> "We stopped treating Chuon Ngo as a heritage site to be preserved and started treating it as a living creative community to be joined. The artisans became collaborators, not subjects. The goal wasn't short-term awareness — it was long-term cultural pride."

**Word count:** 30–60 words

---

### 3.5 `execution` — What You Actually Did

**Format:** Array of 3-5 strings. Each is a specific, verb-led action.

**Rules:**
- Start each bullet with a **strong past-tense verb**: Created, Built, Designed, Partnered, Mapped, Launched, Developed
- Be specific — mention real platforms, formats, numbers where truthful
- Mix tactical actions with human moments
- At least one bullet should show something unexpected or scrappy
- **Never include a bullet that's just "managed social media" or "created content strategy"**

**Pattern per bullet:**
```
[Past-tense verb] + [specific thing] + [human/emotional detail or "not just X" qualifier]
```

**✅ Good examples (from existing projects):**
- "Created TikToks that focused on the reaction moment — not the product, but the person receiving it"
- "30+ in-depth interviews with first-time mothers about their fears, not just their purchases"
- "Partnered with artisans to tell their stories in their own words — no voiceover, no polish"
- "Abandoned cart emails that said 'still looking for the right one? we get it'"
- "A/B tested not just ads, but the stories we told about why people ordered"

**❌ Bad examples (never write these):**
- "Managed social media campaigns across platforms"
- "Developed integrated marketing communications strategy"
- "Created brand awareness through digital channels"
- "Conducted market research and competitive analysis"
- "Optimized conversion funnel across touchpoints"

---

### 3.6 `results` — Honest Outcomes

**Format:** Array of `{ metric: string, value: string }` objects. Always 2-3 entries.

**Rules:**
- Mix **quantitative** (real numbers) with **qualitative** (learning/impact)
- **Always** include a final row with a reflective "What I learned" / "What mattered" / "What changed" metric
- Never fake or inflate numbers
- If the project was a competition, state the placement honestly
- If the project didn't have hard metrics, say what the *intended* impact was

**Pattern:**
```json
[
  { "metric": "[Hard metric if available]", "value": "[Number or achievement]" },
  { "metric": "[Process/outcome metric]", "value": "[What happened]" },
  { "metric": "What I learned", "value": "[Honest, personal reflection in one sentence]" }
]
```

**Template by project type:**

For **real client work** with metrics:
```json
[
  { "metric": "Revenue Growth", "value": "2x" },
  { "metric": "Conversion Rate", "value": "+45%" },
  { "metric": "What worked", "value": "Addressing the anxiety, not just the transaction" }
]
```

For **competitions** without hard metrics:
```json
[
  { "metric": "Champion Title", "value": "Social Pioneers 2025" },
  { "metric": "Workshop Participants", "value": "500+" },
  { "metric": "What actually mattered", "value": "Young people who'd never visited a craft village choosing to go back" }
]
```

For **learning experiences**:
```json
[
  { "metric": "Deliverable", "value": "Completed on time" },
  { "metric": "What I learned", "value": "How the professionals do it. How much I still need to learn." }
]
```

### 3.7 `story` — Deep-Dive Story Blocks (Optional)

**Format:** Array of discriminated union blocks. Only for featured/complex projects.

**Available block types:**
```typescript
| { discriminant: "text";    value: { body: string } }         // Narrative paragraph
| { discriminant: "heading"; value: { body: string } }         // Section header
| { discriminant: "image";   value: { image: string | null;    // Image with caption
                                      caption: string;
                                      size: "content" | "wide" | "full" } }
| { discriminant: "quote";   value: { body: string } }         // Pull quote / insight
| { discriminant: "steps";   value: { heading: string;         // Process steps
                                      items: string[] } }
```

**When to use story blocks:**
- The project has enough depth for 3+ paragraphs of narrative
- There are interesting pivots, failures, or surprises to share
- You want to walk readers through the emotional journey, not just the outcome
- The project is `featured: true`

**Recommended story flow:**
```
1. heading: "The Challenge"
2. text: [expanded challenge narrative, 2-3 paragraphs]
3. image: [context photo, mood board, or research artifact]
4. heading: "The Insight"
5. quote: [the core insight as a pull quote — maximum impact]
6. heading: "The Approach"
7. text: [strategy explanation with rationale]
8. steps: [key execution phases]
9. heading: "The Result"
10. text: [outcome + reflection]
```

---

### 3.8 `awards` — Recognition

**Format:** Array of strings. Include emoji prefix for trophies/medals.

**Pattern:** `"[emoji] [Placement] - [Event Name] [Year]"`

**Examples:**
```json
["🏆 Champion - Social Pioneers 2025"]
["🥈 1st Runner-up - Digital Creatory 2024"]
["Semi-finalist - MOA 2025"]
```

---

### 3.9 `credits` — Team Attribution

**Format:** Array of `{ role: string, name: string }` objects.

**Rules:**
- Always credit collaborators honestly
- If Tien was the sole contributor, leave empty `[]`
- Use standard role titles (Creative Director, Art Director, Copywriter, Strategist, etc.)

**What NOT to copy:**
- Kien is an established award-winner with 10+ agency stamps; he can afford to be minimal because his awards do the talking
- Tien needs *more* narrative because he's earlier-career — the process IS the proof
- Kien's case studies are visual-heavy (he's a copywriter with agency-produced campaigns); Tien's are strategy-heavy

### 4.2 Ho Gia Hoang (hogiahoang.com) — Copywriter at Tombras (US)

**What Hoang does well:**

- **Clean project titles** — memorable campaign names ("Fresh like the ocean")

- **Personal voice in About** — "Think of me as the bridge between sriracha and ranch dressing"

**Hoang's Case Study Pattern:**
```

2. Campaign title (creative, not descriptive)
3. Agency credits (structured list)
4. Campaign video embed
5. Supporting visuals / GIFs

```

**What to borrow:**
- The warm, specific personal hooks
- Clean credit blocks with proper role hierarchy
- Separating "real work" from "school/spec work" if applicable
- Not over-explaining — trust the visuals when you have them

**What NOT to copy:**
- Hoang is US-based with VCU Brandcenter degree and agency backing; his work is TVC/campaign-heavy
- His case studies have almost NO written narrative (just credits + visuals) — this only works because his campaigns are self-explanatory
- Tien's work is more strategy/insight-heavy — needs more text, more "why"

### 4.3 VCU Brandcenter Style

**Pattern from top Brandcenter graduates:**
```
1. Compelling campaign title
2. One-line problem statement
3. The insight (bridge from problem to idea)
4. The big idea (one sentence)
5. Show the execution (visuals, scripts, mockups)
6. Results if available
```

**What to borrow:**
- The "insight → big idea" architecture is gold
- Tight, punchy writing — every word earns its place
- Campaign names that are memorable, not descriptive

### 4.4 Key Differences: What Tien Should Do Differently

| Aspect | Kien/Hoang (established) | Tien (early career) |
|--------|--------------------------|---------------------|
| Text vs. visuals | ~20% text, ~80% visual | ~50% text, ~50% visual |
| Awards prominence | Lead with awards | Lead with the question/insight |
| Case study depth | Brief — work speaks for itself | Deep — process IS the proof |
| Reflection/learning | Rarely shown | Always shown — it's a strength |
| Cultural voice | Embedded naturally | Same — but lean in more |

## 5. ANTI-PATTERNS — WHAT MAKES PORTFOLIO WRITING BAD

### Hard Rules (Never Break These)

| # | Anti-Pattern | Why It's Bad | Tien's Alternative |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Opening with metrics** | "Increased engagement by 300%" tells nothing about thinking | Open with the human struggle |
| 2 | **Marketing jargon** | "Leveraged omnichannel synergies" = textbook, not thinking | Plain language. Short sentences. |
| 3 | **Passive voice** | "A campaign was developed" — by who? | "We built…" / "I designed…" |
| 4 | **Generic descriptions** | "Created engaging content for social media" = nothing | Be specific: "Created TikToks of unboxing reactions" |
| 5 | **Over-claiming** | "Revolutionized the industry" — no you didn't | "Shifted the approach from X to Y" |
| 6 | **No reflection** | Lists tasks without learning = resume, not portfolio | Always add "What I learned" |
| 7 | **Explaining the obvious** | "Social media is important for brands today" | Jump to the insight |
| 8 | **AI slop tells** | "In today's fast-paced digital landscape…" | Delete the entire sentence |
| 9 | **Fake humility** | "I was just a small part of the team" | Be clear about your role. Own it. |
| 10 | **Wall of text** | No one reads 500-word paragraphs in a portfolio | 2-3 sentences per paragraph max |

### AI-Specific Tells to NEVER Use

These phrases **immediately signal AI-generated content**. Absolute blacklist:

- "In today's [anything]…"
- "It's worth noting that…"
- "At the end of the day…"
- "This project was a testament to…"
- "Leveraging [anything]"
- "Delve into…"
- "Holistic approach"
- "Synergy" / "synergistic"
- "Game-changer"
- "Cutting-edge"
- "Best-in-class"
- "Seamlessly" / "seamless"
- "Unlock" (as in "unlock potential")
- "Elevate" (unless quoting someone)
- "Foster" (as in "foster engagement")
- "Robust"
- "Landscape" (as in "marketing landscape")
- "Navigate" (as in "navigate challenges")
- "Empower"
- "Curate" (as a verb in marketing context)
- "Resonate" (overused AI tell)
- "Tapestry"
- "Multifaceted"

## 6. THE DATA SCHEMA — TECHNICAL REFERENCE

When writing projects, the output must fit this TypeScript interface from `content/projects.ts`:

```typescript
interface Project {
  slug: string;           // URL-safe project identifier (kebab-case)
  title: string;          // Project name (proper case, brand-first)
  tagline: string;        // The emotional hook (question/statement)
  client: string;         // Client or competition name
  brand?: string;         // Brand name if different from client
  industry: string;       // Industry category
  role: string;           // Tien's specific role (use "&" for dual roles)
  year: string;           // Year of project
  thumbnail: string | null;  // Path: /images/projects/items/{N}/thumbnail.{ext}
  heroImage: string | null;  // Path: /images/projects/items/{N}/heroImage.{ext}
  featured: boolean;      // true = shows on homepage, gets story blocks
  color: string;          // Hex color for project accent
  story?: StoryBlock[];   // Optional deep-dive narrative blocks
  challenge?: string;     // The reframed problem (40-80 words)
  insight?: string;       // The cultural/emotional truth (15-40 words)
  approach?: string;      // The strategic shift (30-60 words)
  execution?: string[];   // 3-5 specific action bullets
  results?: { metric: string; value: string }[];  // 2-3 metric/learning pairs
  awards?: string[];      // Award placements with emoji
  credits?: { role: string; name: string }[];      // Team credits
  gallery?: GalleryItem[];
}

type StoryBlock =
  | { discriminant: "text"; value: { body: string } }
  | { discriminant: "heading"; value: { body: string } }
  | { discriminant: "image"; value: { image: string | null; caption: string; size: "content" | "wide" | "full" } }
  | { discriminant: "quote"; value: { body: string } }
  | { discriminant: "steps"; value: { heading: string; items: string[] } };

interface GalleryItem {
  type: "image" | "video" | "videoFile";
  image: string | null;
  videoUrl: string;
  videoFile: string | null;
  size: "full" | "half";
  caption: string;
}
```

## 8. QUALITY CHECKLIST — BEFORE PUBLISHING

Run every project through this checklist:

- [ ] **Tagline** is a question or emotional statement, not a project description
- [ ] **Challenge** reframes the brief from business to human terms
- [ ] **Insight** is one sentence that makes you go "oh, that's true"
- [ ] **Approach** uses "stopped…started" or "instead of…we" framing
- [ ] **Execution** bullets start with strong verbs and include human details
- [ ] **Results** include at least one "What I learned" / "What mattered" row
- [ ] **No marketing jargon** anywhere
- [ ] **No AI slop phrases** (check against the banned list in Section 5)
- [ ] **Voice sounds like Tien** — conversational, specific, humble-confident
- [ ] **Word count** is reasonable (challenge: 40-80w, insight: 15-40w, approach: 30-60w)
- [ ] **Cultural details** are present where natural (Vietnamese phrases, local references)
- [ ] **Role is clear** — reader knows exactly what Tien did vs. what the team did
- [ ] **Reflection is honest** — includes what didn't work or what was learned

## 10. ADAPTING FOR DIFFERENT PROJECT TYPES

### Competition Projects (Social Pioneers, Digital Creatory, MOA)
- Emphasis on **strategic thinking** and **insight quality**
- Results = placement + what you'd measure if it were real
- Challenge should reference the competition brief but humanize it
- Credits for team members
- Tone: slightly more polished but still personal

### Client Work (VuVer, AEON, Red Agency)
- Emphasis on **real outcomes** and **execution details**
- Results = actual metrics where available
- Challenge should reference real business context
- Be careful about confidentiality — check with client before publishing specifics
- Tone: professional-casual, more "we" than "I"

### Personal Ventures (Pixself)
- Emphasis on **entrepreneurial learning** and **why you started**
- Results = honest numbers + personal growth reflection
- Challenge = your own hypothesis vs. what you discovered
- Most freedom with voice — can be most personal
- Tone: most casual, most self-reflective

### Learning Experiences (AEON TVC)
- Emphasis on **humility** and **observation**
- Results = what you learned, not what you delivered
- Challenge = what you were there to do (support role, not lead)
- Shortest case study — 2 paragraphs is enough
- Shows maturity: "not every project is about being the star"
- Tone: grateful, observational, growth-oriented
